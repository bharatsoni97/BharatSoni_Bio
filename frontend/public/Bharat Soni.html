About

I'm an economic and strategic advisor with a background in financial and business economics and five years of experience applying that training across government and consulting. I've worked inside a provincial ministry designing policy programs for a $2.6B budget, and at KPMG advising firms across financial services, energy, utilities, and mining on how economic forces shape operations and strategy. My work sits at the intersection of macroeconomic analysis and operational reality — where the insight only matters if it changes the decision. I'm building Econometrics.AI to extend that practice: giving businesses direct access to the quantitative economic analysis that typically lives inside advisory engagements. I hold three degrees from Schulich and York University, including an accelerated MBA in progress, and have twice represented York at the Bank of Canada Governor's Challenge.
